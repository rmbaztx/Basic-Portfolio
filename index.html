About Me

I was born in Connecticut, but my family moved to Dallas when I was seven years old. I have since lived in Phoenix, Arizona and Austin, Texas. My wife, Cindy and I have three children and two grandchildren. I began my career "fixing" things -- first aircraft instruments, then process automation equipment, followed by CAD/CAM systems, computer-controlled machine tools and what became known as information technology. I worked for Honeywell International for 32 years with roles in field service, management, marketing, and business transformation. The broad exposure to many industries and manufacturing processes was very enjoyable. I am the type of guy who would watch a television show called "How It Is Made;" just naturally curious. Working for a large corporation was great for me at that time in my life, as I was able to learn something new every day and was not only exposed to methodologies like Lean Six Sigma, but was able to become an "expert" at many disciplines. I am a life-long learner. My hobbies include golf, playing guitar, and motorcycling.

The only downside to my career was the extensive travel it required. I was away from my family a lot. While I enjoyed seeing so much in so many different places and working with awesome folks from many countries and cultures, I always felt that I should be closer to home.
